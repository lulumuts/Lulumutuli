This project was funded by DANIDA and Designing Africa Collective, and took place over a period of 6 months of design and fabric development. The project objectives were to create a new textile for a new collection, to tackle the problem of accessing variety fabrics in Kenya. Afro-glitch is hence, a sample collection consisting of 7 looks and 12 pieces that transition between fabrics and prints that are inspired by the natural textures of the Earth and traditional garments of various areas in Continental Africa. These are juxtaposed against the digital and silky smooth prints that represent the technological boom taking place within Kenya and the Diaspora. It is the first collection under the brand name “AMILO” which is a brand dedicated to creating Afrofuturistic inspired pieces for the modern working woman within Africa and the diaspora.
